Abstract Submission .

POSTER PRESENTATION/COMPETITION.

Abstract submission deadline: April 30th 2019
Abstract acceptance notification: July 31th, 2019

ABSTRACT RULES AND GUIDELINES.

1. Individuals may present only one abstract.
2. Individuals can co-author multiple abstracts.
3. Projects being submitted must be original research.
4. Only abstracts written in English will be accepted.
5. The abstract should be 300 words or less with a Title limits to 10 words.
6. After the title, list the author’s names and institutional affiliations
7. Please capitalize the entire title and underline the name of the author who will present the paper.
8. The text should be typed using single spacing. Do not indent the title and each paragraph.
9. Each abstract is required to have an Objective(s), Method(s), Result(s), and Conclusion(s) section.

10.Objective(s):
Please clearly state the objective(s) of the study. Background of the study is not required or should be kept very concise.

11.Method(s):
Please describe the methods used including study design, study population and sampling, key outcome variables, research tool, data collection and data analysis employed.

12.Result(s):
Please report the essential results, including data and statistics (where appropriate)

13.Conclusion(s):
Please conclude based on the objectives. Recommendation is not required or should be kept very concise.

14. You are allowed to edit your abstract until the submission is closed.



ABSTRACT ACCEPTANCE.

1. Each abstract will be reviewed so that high standards can be ensured. A panel of reviewers will select the abstracts on the basis of the scientific quality of the work as judged from the abstract.

2.All poster presenters must register for the main conference upon acceptance of abstract. Last date for registration will be on 10 th August 2018.

3. The following are common reasons for rejection. This is provided to call your attention to points that should be avoided.
   a) Abstract is not original research.
   b) Nature of problem not explicit from either title or abstract.
   c) Material too closely related to another abstract submitted by the same co- authors;
   should have been combined into a single paper.
   d) Abstract has been presented at other meeting(s) or previously published.
   e) Abstract is not complete and poorly organized. Required information not given in the abstract.
   f) Study methods are not adequately described or not appropriate to achieve the study objectives.
   g) Significance of results related to the problem being studied is not stated.
   h) Conclusions do not answer the study objectives.
